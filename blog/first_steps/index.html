First Steps

Posted: Jan 21/15

https://www.pinterest.com/shawn1526/programmer-memes/

The above picture pretty much sums up most of my programming expereince. A lot of

frustrating nights staring at an array of pixels on a screen representing some mess of code I

hacked the previous night. Sometimes the code won't do what I thought, and sometimes it would.

Most of my free time educating myself on programming computers felt more like teaching myself

how to use a fucking search engine! 


The constant battle of a self-taught programmer is fought on a very old plain polluted with

dying technologies, poorly designed tutorials and the rotten stench of biasies. Everyone on the

internet seems to know what's best and most relevent to learn. But then everyone else says

everyone else is full of kaka.


It's easy as a noob to get lost and missguided in the world of programming, especially if

you're working a full-time job that's NOT programming to boot. This has been my battle for 3

years now, and I plan to change it.


I can't say I have achieved my goal of working in the industry, and I'm not even at a level of

confidence to participate in open-source programs. However, through a great struggle I have

found many resourses and learned a lot about "learning" how to program. Where to get started,

what kind of advice works and doesn't work. I wish to share everything I know with anyone

venturing out into the ever growing learning curve of computers. While sharing what I have

already learned, I hope to become better at what I am knowledgeable in and further develop my

skills to hopefully land a job as a full-time programmer. 


I'm going to start taking action towards a career in software development. So this is my first

attempt at doing something productive with my few, but hard earned skills. I plan to build a

website to host all of my programs, games, applications - just whatever I make really. This blog

will be a way for me to share what I have learned along the way.


~Dan Fletcher
